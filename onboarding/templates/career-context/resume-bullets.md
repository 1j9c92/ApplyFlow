## How to Use This File

1. **When tailoring a resume**: Filter by the skills most relevant to the target role
2. **When writing a cover letter**: Find bullets that demonstrate the capabilities the job posting emphasizes
3. **When prepping for interviews**: Reference these accomplishments when asked about your experience
4. **When updating your resume**: Pick the strongest bullets for that job entry

---

## Skill Tags Reference

Use consistent tags across all accomplishments so you can filter easily. Examples:

- **Strategic**: Strategy, planning, roadmapping, competitive analysis
- **Financial**: Budgeting, forecasting, financial analysis, P&L, unit economics
- **Analytical**: Data analysis, SQL, modeling, research, quantitative thinking
- **Operations**: Process improvement, systems building, automation, efficiency
- **Leadership**: Team management, mentoring, cross-functional leadership
- **Communication**: Writing, presenting, stakeholder management, clarity
- **Technical**: Engineering, architecture, technical problem-solving
- **Product**: Product strategy, roadmapping, user-centered thinking
- **Growth**: Revenue, scaling, demand generation, customer acquisition
- **Business Development**: Partnerships, deal structuring, sales

---

## Strength Rating Guide

- **★★★★★**: Exceptional accomplishment with clear metrics. Shows rare or high-demand capability. Use these for dream roles.
- **★★★★**: Strong accomplishment with solid evidence. Clearly demonstrates capability. Use liberally.
- **★★★**: Solid accomplishment. Good contextual fit for specific roles or industries. Use when relevant.
- **★★**: Niche accomplishment or fewer metrics. Use only when specifically relevant to role.
